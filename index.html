STAR Stories

Key project experiences using the STAR method.

STAR Story 1 – Embrace Adaptability and Resiliency

Situation: At the beginning of my project, I was uncertain which IT career path—database design or IT support—best aligned with my strengths. The range of possible directions felt overwhelming, and I needed clarity to move forward with confidence.

Task: My goal was to research both career paths, identify at least ten essential industry skills, and analyze real job postings to understand expectations, gaps, and opportunities—especially as I adapted to new information along the way.

Action: I embraced adaptability by exploring both paths simultaneously, adjusting my research focus as I discovered new tools, responsibilities, and skill requirements. I reviewed job descriptions, LinkedIn profiles, and industry articles, documenting patterns and skill trends. As my understanding evolved, I refined my comparisons, identified gaps in my own skill set, and realigned my goals without losing momentum.

Result: I produced a comprehensive research package outlining role expectations, required skills, and career trends. By staying resilient through uncertainty and adapting to new insights, I clarified my direction and confidently selected IT help desk and database roles as my starting targets. This adaptability reduced career-planning stress and set a clear foundation for the rest of my project.

STAR Story 2 – Be a Diligent, Respectful, and Caring Steward

Situation: My research showed that my SQL, data modeling, and troubleshooting skills were not strong enough for many entry-level roles. To respect the expectations of the field and responsibly steward my own professional growth, I needed to strengthen these areas.

Task: I set a goal to learn at least three new skills and significantly improve my knowledge of at least ten technical competencies identified in my research. I approached this goal with diligence and a commitment to building skills the right way, not just quickly.

Action: I completed LinkedIn Learning and Alison courses, took detailed notes, and practiced consistently to ensure I was building accurate, high-quality skills. I treated each resource with care—documenting lessons, reflecting on progress, and ensuring I respected best practices in database design, troubleshooting, cybersecurity, and networking. I also created small SQL projects to apply what I learned, reinforcing stewardship of both my learning process and the technical concepts themselves.

Result: By the end of this phase, I mastered more than ten technical skills and gained at least three new ones, including advanced SQL, troubleshooting methodology, and cloud fundamentals. My diligent and respectful approach to learning strengthened my confidence, improved my technical foundation, and demonstrated that I can be a responsible and caring steward of both my work and the systems I support.

STAR Story 3 – Navigate Complexity

Situation: Many IT and database positions listed certifications as a key requirement. With numerous certification options, varied course structures, and overlapping topics, the path forward felt complex. I needed to strategically choose and complete certifications that aligned with job expectations.

Task: My goal was to earn multiple free Alison certifications related to database concepts and IT technician skills by the project deadline—choosing courses that would meaningfully strengthen my professional credibility.

Action: To navigate the complexity of course choices and technical content, I mapped each certification to specific job skills identified in my earlier research. I enrolled in two Alison courses—Introduction to Database Concepts and Essentials of IT Technician Skills—and completed all modules, quizzes, and assessments. I organized key concepts such as SQL structure, cloud computing, disaster recovery, and networking basics, ensuring I understood how each topic fit into the broader IT landscape.

Result: I earned two certifications with scores of 80% and 88%. By navigating the complexity of technical topics and certification requirements, I strengthened my resume and demonstrated my ability to manage multifaceted tasks. These credentials also validated my skills and enhanced my readiness for entry-level IT and database roles.

STAR Story 4 - Creating My Career Tools & Progress Website

Situation: Before this project, my resume, LinkedIn profile, and professional branding were outdated and didn’t reflect my new IT and database skills. I needed a cohesive professional presentation to appeal to employers.

Task: I set a goal to redesign my resume, rewrite my LinkedIn profile, create an elevator pitch, and develop a professional website showcasing my progress and qualifications.

Action: I updated my resume with new technical skills, certifications, and project experience. I rewrote my LinkedIn headline, About section, and skills, and added new coursework and accomplishments. I wrote a clear elevator pitch and built a six-section website documenting my research, learning, certifications, interviews, and applications. I included screenshots, explanations, and real progress indicators.

Result: I created a complete professional toolkit ready for employers. My resume is targeted and polished, my LinkedIn profile is aligned with my skills and goals, and my website provides an in-depth look at my qualifications. These improvements significantly enhanced my professional presence and prepared me for job applications and networking.

STAR Story 5 - Applying for Entry-Level Data-Focused Roles

Situation: Toward the end of my project, one of my goals was to begin applying for real entry-level IT or data roles. However, I faced challenges: many positions were too far away, required experience I did not yet have, or had technical requirements beyond my current skill level. Despite these obstacles, I still needed to complete the application deliverable with roles that realistically matched my abilities and geographic range.

Task: My objective was to find legitimate, attainable job opportunities and submit professional, targeted applications-even if the number of suitable openings was limited. I aimed to demonstrate readiness by completing at least two strong applications aligned with my developing data skills.

Action: I carefully reviewed many job postings and identified two realistic roles that matched both my skill set and location constraints:
1. A Data Entry Assistant position emphasizing accuracy, typing speed, and attention to detail.
2. A Data Analyst role at East West Bank, which aligned with my SQL practice and interest in analytics. I tailored my resume and cover letter for each role, completed typing tests, and documented the application submissions with confirmation screenshots.

Result: Despite a limited pool of roles that met my criteria, I successfully submitted two high-quality applications aligned with my skills and career direction. Completing these applications strengthened my job-search confidence, demonstrated professional readiness, and clarified the expectations of data-focused roles. This experience also highlighted areas for continued improvement as I move toward full-time employment in IT or data.
